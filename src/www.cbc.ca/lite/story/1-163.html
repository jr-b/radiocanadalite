What's the latest situation for the London area?

The Middlesex-London Health Unit (MLHU) reported 51 new COVID-19 cases and no deaths since Friday. The tally includes 21 cases reported Saturday, 18 reported Sunday and 12 on Monday.

The three-day results came after 1,141 people were tested for the virus at the Carling Heights assessment centre.

The health unit posted 59 recoveries since Friday, with 169 known active cases in the region. The rolling seven-day average of daily cases fell to 18.

Embed | Miscellaneous

London Health Sciences Centre says it is treating 13 patients with COVID-19-related illnesses. There are six patients in adult critical care, and five or fewer in the Children's Hospital and Paediatric Critical Care.

Five or fewer staff members at the hospital network have tested positive for the virus and are in quarantine.

COVID-19 cases at London schools

Three additional COVID-19 outbreaks were declared at London-area schools over the weekend. There are now five active outbreaks:

     
  • École élémentaire La Pommeraie (declared Sept. 17 )
    •  
  • St. Thomas More Catholic School (declared Sept. 22)
    •  
  • Ekcoe Central School (declared Sept. 25)
    •  
  • Lord Elgin Public School (declared Sept. 26)
    •  
  • Wilfrid Jury Public School (declared Sept. 26)
    •  

Those impacted by the outbreaks have been contacted directly and instructed to isolate at home.

There have now been 66 cases of COVID-19 reported at London-area schools and four cases at child care centres in September.
