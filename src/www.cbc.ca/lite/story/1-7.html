New COVID-19 projections coming as Ontario confirms 466 cases

The Canadian Press | Posted: Tuesday, September 28, 2021, 12:25 PM UTC | Last Updated: 1 hour ago

Chief medical officer of health's briefing now scheduled for 3:30 p.m. ET on Wednesday

Image | Miscellaneous

Caption: Paramedics with Ornge ambulance service load a patient outside Scarborough General Hospital, in north-east Toronto, earlier this year. (Evan Mitsui/CBC)

Ontario confirmed 466 new COVID-19 cases on Tuesday and 11 more deaths among those with the illness.

There were 819 new recoveries.

There are 315 people in hospital with COVID-19, and 180 of those were in intensive care.

Of the 315 in hospital with a known vaccination status, 235 were unvaccinated.

Some 94 per cent of those in the ICU were either unvaccinated or partially vaccinated, according to provincial data. 

The daily update comes as Ontario's science advisory table is set to release new COVID-19 projections, which will be posted online and in this story.

Meanwhile, Ontario's chief medical officer of health was expected to hold his weekly briefing today but this has now been rescheduled to Wednesday, Sept. 29.

Ontario's daily case counts have so far remained under 1,000 during the fourth wave, and the graph of Ontario's seven-day average roughly shows a plateau since the beginning of September.

That's well under the worst-case scenario in Ontario's previous modelling, which showed there could be some 4,000 daily cases by now.

Reality is more in line with the best-case scenario, in which cases would have steadily fallen since Sept. 1.
