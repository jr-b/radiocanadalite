Man rushed to hospital after stabbing in Winnipeg

CBC News | Posted: Tuesday, September 28, 2021, 12:16 PM UTC | Last Updated: Just now

Police called just before 4 a.m. to corner of Dufferin Avenue and Robinson Street

Image | Miscellaneous

Caption: Police cruisers guard the scene of a stabbing on Tuesday morning. (Meaghan Ketcheson/CBC)

Police tape surrounded a convenience store in Winnipeg's North End on Tuesday morning after a stabbing sent a man to hospital.

Emergency crews were called to the store, at the corner of Dufferin Avenue and Robinson Street, just before 4 a.m.

A man in his 30s was rushed to hospital in critical condition and later upgraded to stable, police said.

Investigators believe the man was attacked during an altercation outside the store. A bystander intervened and the attacker ran off.

No arrests have been made, police said.

Anyone with information is asked to call investigators at 204-986-6219 or Crime Stoppers at 204-786-TIPS (8477).
