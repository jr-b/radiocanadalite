COVID-19 outbreak declared at 3 Saskatoon elementary schools

CBC News | Posted: Monday, September 27, 2021, 12:43 PM UTC | Last Updated: September 27

Classes will continue at Chief Whitecap, Ernest Lindner and W.P. Bate Schools

Image | Miscellaneous

Caption: The Saskatchewan Health Authority declared COVID-19 outbreaks at three Saskatoon elementary schools on Sunday. (Warren Kay/CBC)

On Sunday, the Saskatchewan Health Authority declared outbreaks at three elementary schools in Saskatoon.

The health authority said there were outbreaks at Chief Whitecap, Ernest Lindner and W.P. Bate Schools.

Since the beginning of school, there have been nine cases of COVID-19 at Chief Whitecap School, six cases at Ernest Lindner School and three cases at W.P. Bate School.

All of the schools will resume classes as normal on Monday.

Saskatoon Public Schools said it had notified students, staff and families at each of the schools.

Since the beginning of the school year, there have been 100 positive cases at 38 schools within the division, according to a news release from the division.

The public school division said it would continue enforcing indoor mask rules and enhanced cleaning. It encouraged all eligible students, staff and parents to get the COVID-19 vaccine.
