6 people in hospital with serious or critical injuries after Saturday's student parties

CBC News | Posted: Monday, September 27, 2021, 7:48 PM UTC | Last Updated: 17 hours ago

Paramedics say they got 68 calls and had to take 30 people to hospital on Saturday evening and Sunday morning

Image | Miscellaneous

Caption: Still images from the account Canadian Party Life show young people around Western University in large gatherings and jumping off roofs and climbing poles. (Supplied by Instagram/Canadian Party Life)

Six people were sent to hospital with serious or critical injuries after Saturday's wild parties around Western University, with dozens of people facing charges for being a public nuisance, jumping off roofs and urinating in public. 

While police kept revellers primarily calm and quiet during the day — Middlesex London Paramedic Service was called seven times and took two people to hospital between 8 a.m. and 5 p.m. on Saturday — things got significantly more dangerous in the evening and overnight. 

"(We) saw an influx of calls for services in the Broughdale area from 5 p.m. to 3 a.m., which warranted an additional 68 calls for paramedics and resulted in 30 people being transported to hospital with varying issues," said spokesperson Miranda Bothwell. 

"Six of those patients would have been classified as serious/critical."

Embed | Miscellaneous

About 2,000 people were at parties on Huron Street and surrounding areas near the university, police said. Broughdale Avenue, one block north of Huron and the past notorious site of massive gatherings, was closed by police for the day. 

"While there were no major incidents to report, the event was a significant strain on emergency services throughout the city of London," police said in a statement.

London police issued statistics for only their daytime enforcement on Saturday, and while there were large parties into the night, the statistics on those are not available, police said. 

The following summarizes the charges, arrests and tickets issued between 7 a.m. and 7 p.m.:

     
  • 20 Provincial Offence Act Notices issued under the Liquor Licence Act;

  • 66 Administrative Monetary Penalties (AMPs) were issued or are being processed for parking violations. Three vehicles were towed;

  • 12 AMPs are being processed for the following offences: noise, public nuisance party attendance, use of a closed street, and public urination;
    •  
  • 3 AMPs will be issued to individuals under the Public Nuisance By-law for being on roofs;
    •  
  • Zero Criminal Code charges; and
    •  
  • One arrest where the person was warned and released without charges.

Police also released statistics from Project LEARN, the annual Liquor Enforcement and Reduction of Noise campaign to encourage students to "act responsibly."

Between Sept. 5 and Sept. 25, the enforcement blitz resulted in one charge under the Reopening Ontario Act, 34 provincial offence notices, 12 bylaw notices and three arrests for being drunk in a public place. Those statistics don't include the enforcement conducted around Broughdale Avenue and surrounding streets on Saturday.

Western responds

In a written statement, Western University said the street parties don't represent the school's values or those of its students. 

"There is no excuse for breaking the law, taxing emergency response professionals and damaging public property," it read, adding that the dangerous events put extra pressure on frontline workers. The school thanked EMS workers for their commitment to keeping students safe. 

The statement reminded students who attended the events to visit the on-campus testing centre if they're experiencing COVID-19 symptoms.
