Students from India bound for northern Ontario relieved with lifting of direct flight ban

Erik White | CBC News | Posted: Monday, September 27, 2021, 10:53 AM UTC | Last Updated: September 27

Direct flights were banned in April when COVID-19 cases in India were climbing rapidly

Image | Miscellaneous

Caption: Effy Dehlvi, 30, is more confident he'll be able to travel from India to Sudbury in October to begin studies at Cambrian College. (Effy Dehlvi)

Students from India who were bound for schools in northern Ontario are relieved the Canadian government is now allowing direct flights.

Since April, anyone travelling to and from India had to stop in another country along the way, often at great expense and the stress of possibly being stranded in a strange land.

Effy Dehlvi is a 30-year-old still in India, but who's set to begin studies in international business management at Cambrian College in Sudbury in November, at a cost of $16,000.

He said this has been a nerve-wracking time for him and other Indian students bound for Canada. He made sure to get two shots of AstraZeneca, the only COVID-19 vaccine available in India that is recognized by Canada.

"Because this is something really unpredictable," said Dehlvi The students over here are really nervous about what's going to happen and what's not going to happen. We are just hoping for the best." 

He said he agrees with the thought that the Canadian government seems to be treating India differently than other countries with similar COVID-19 outbreaks. 

Image | Cambrian College

Caption: Several Indian students attending Cambrian College and other northern Ontario schools this fall had their travel plans disrupted by the direct flight ban. (Erik White/CBC)

"Well, to be honest, yes," said Dehlvi. 

"Because I think they should include every country, not just India. Because the people in Canada are coming from all over the world."

The direct flight ban was also difficult for members of Indian community in northern Ontario.

Pinakin Patel is a Canadian citizen who has lived in Sudbury for the past six years and owns the Subway restaurant in the city's downtown.

The 47-year-old returned to India in March to look after his ailing father, just as COVID-19 cases were skyrocketing in his home country. 

"We didn't go there for vacation. Our loved ones, they need us," said Patel.

Image | Pinakin Patel

Caption: Pinakin Patel, 47, went home to India to be with his ailing father in the spring. He says it took him a week to get back to Sudbury with the flight restrictions. (Pinakin Patel)

Patel said he was "lucky" to be in Indian when his father died in June.

The journey back to Canada with the direct flight ban took almost a week with stops in the Netherlands and Mexico and multiple COVID-19 tests.

"It is good that now they lift the ban," said Patel.

"The trouble I face, I don't want anybody else to face the same trouble."
